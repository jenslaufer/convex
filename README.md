# It's Just Math

An interactive, single-file web essay on **convexity, compounding, and non-ergodicity** — for anyone who thinks in geometric returns.

**Live:** https://jenslaufer.github.io/its-just-math/

Three interactive pieces:

1. **The non-ergodic coin toss** — positive expected value, negative time-average growth. The Peters/Taleb counter-example to naive EV-maximization.
2. **The volatility tax** — why drawdowns cost more than equal-sized gains return, and how variance drags compound growth below the arithmetic mean.
3. **Convexity** — a crash hedge that loses ~20%/yr standalone, allocated at a small weight, *raises* compound growth while *lowering* the arithmetic mean — ending with multiples of the wealth over a long horizon. Move the payoff multiple and crash probability to watch the optimum appear and vanish.

## Sources

- Ole Peters, *"The ergodicity problem in economics,"* Nature Physics 15 (2019).
- Mark Spitznagel, *Safe Haven* (2021).

The mathematics is exact and reproducible.
